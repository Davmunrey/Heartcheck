# When NOT to use HeartScan

HeartScan is an **educational** tool. It does **not** diagnose, triage or replace clinical care. Do not use HeartScan in any of the following situations.

## Emergency

- Acute chest pain, pressure, or burning.
- Sudden shortness of breath, syncope, or palpitations with dizziness.
- Suspected stroke (face droop, weakness, slurred speech, vision changes).
- Any "I feel something is very wrong right now" moment.

→ Call your local emergency number (112 / 911 / equivalent) immediately.

## Known cardiac history

- Diagnosed arrhythmia under treatment.
- Implanted devices (pacemakers, ICDs, loop recorders).
- Recent cardiac surgery, ablation, or stent.
- Hereditary syndromes (long QT, Brugada, hypertrophic cardiomyopathy, etc.).

→ Follow your cardiologist's monitoring plan. HeartScan's signal is too coarse to add value here.

## Pregnancy or paediatric use

- The classifier is not validated on pregnant patients or on children. Heart rate norms and rhythm patterns differ; expect inflated false-positive and false-negative rates.

## Clinical settings

- Inpatient monitoring, pre-operative assessment, or any decision that affects patient management. Use a properly certified ECG device with appropriate quality assurance.

## Marketing or compliance contexts

- HeartScan must never appear in marketing copy as a diagnostic device. See [`docs/legal/DRAFT_NOTICE.md`](legal/DRAFT_NOTICE.md).
- For regulated jurisdictions (EU MDR, FDA SaMD), HeartScan as currently shipped does not meet the bar for a medical device.

## What the product *can* help with

- Curiosity ("what does this strip roughly look like?") with the disclaimer in mind.
- Education about ECG basics (rhythm, regularity).
- Triggering a "should I get this checked?" conversation with a clinician.

The result is an **orientation**, not a verdict. A green status is not reassurance; a red status is not a diagnosis. When in doubt, talk to a healthcare professional.
